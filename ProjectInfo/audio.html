NW Audio

NWAudio.java

Initially I (Michael) did not intend on coding my own audio applet. I wanted to find one on the net and use it in NW. The problem was that the more I searched, the more I was displeased with the audio applets I found. So with some encouragement from Rasmus, I decided to code my own.

After a short while, I thought I had an audio applet... No sound, though. It turns out that our samples were 22kHz instead of 8kHz and in aifc format instead of au format.... So with some help debugging from Rasmus, I eventually produced the NWAudio.java applet.

Right now this applet accepts two parameters from the web page, duration and sound file. Duration tells whether or not to loop the sound, and the sound variable contains the sound file to be played.

SoundLib.java

In addition to the audio applet found on the web pages, Rasmus coded an audio class into the one player game in order to produce our infamous sound effects. He didn't have as much trouble coding his class as I did with my applet...

Audio Samples

The samples found in NW come from the PC game "Beavis and Butt-head in Virtual Stupidity". This game was awesome and rich in sound samples. For test purposes, we decided to use the samples for MUMS evaluation only. In no way do we take credit for these samples. The samples are under copyright of Viacom International, Inc. and we are attempting to contact them for permission to use their samples in NW since this is for non-profit purposes.

We have edited the samples in order to keep them in context with the game, and have reduced their quality (from 22kHz to 8kHz) in order to play them in our Java audio clip code.
